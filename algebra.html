Simplification Using Boolean Algebra

What Is Boolean Algebra?

Boolean algebra is a branch of algebra that was formulated by George Boole. It involves the manipulation of variables that can only be one of two values. Either true or false(0 or 1). Using it we can simplify complex boolean expressions and once simplified, can implement them into logic circuits which are used in electronics.

How It Works

Boolean algebra operates somewhat differently from traditional algebra, and operations like addition and subtraction aren't really referenced in the same way. Instead, the main 3 fundemental operations of boolean algebra are conjunctiuon, disjunction, and negation. These take the form of AND, OR and NOT gates in our electrical circuits. Proper application and manipulation of these operations allows us to simplify any boolean expression, no matter the complexity.
